## 1. Project Overview
This Project Charter formally authorizes the establishment of an
Information Security Governance, Risk and Compliance (GRC) program
for Novalink Technologies Ltd. This engagement is being executed by
an external GRC Consulting Firm on behalf of Novalink's executive
leadership.

Novalink Technologies Ltd is a Financial Technology company
headquartered in Lagos, Nigeria with operations in Ghana and Kenya.
The company provides digital payment solutions and mobile banking
services to consumers and small businesses across West and East Africa.

---

## 2. Business Justification
Novalink currently operates at a Low security maturity level with no
formal security policies, risk registers or documented controls. As a
licensed Fintech operating under the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)
regulatory framework, Novalink is legally obligated to implement and
maintain a robust information security program.

The following business drivers justify this engagement:

- Regulatory Obligation: CBN Cybersecurity Framework and NDPR require
  formal security governance structures and documented controls
- Business Risk: Novalink handles sensitive financial and personal data
  across three countries exposing it to significant cyber risk
- Growth Ambition: Novalink plans to expand operations and attract
  institutional investors who require evidence of security maturity
- Reputational Risk: A security breach in a Fintech environment causes
  irreparable damage to customer trust and brand reputation
- Competitive Advantage: A strong GRC program differentiates Novalink
  from competitors in the African Fintech market

---

## 3. Project Objectives
By the end of this engagement Novalink will have:

1. A formally documented security governance structure including
   org chart and defined roles and responsibilities
2. A comprehensive RACI Matrix defining accountability across all
   key security activities
3. A set of core security policies aligned to ISO 27001 and CBN
   Cybersecurity Framework requirements
4. A technically validated security baseline established through
   vulnerability scanning using Nessus and Nmap
5. A remediation roadmap based on identified vulnerabilities and
   compliance gaps

---

## 4. Project Scope

### In Scope
- Security governance framework design
- Risk and compliance documentation
- Security policy development
- Technical vulnerability assessment using Nessus and Nmap
- Remediation recommendations based on scan findings
- All three Novalink locations — Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya

### Out of Scope
- Physical security assessments — requires separate specialist engagement
- Penetration testing and exploitation — vulnerability assessment only
  is included. Active exploitation of identified weaknesses is excluded
- Implementation of technical controls — the consultant identifies and
  recommends. Novalink's IT team is responsible for implementation
- Software development or system configuration changes
- Legal representation or regulatory filing on behalf of Novalink
