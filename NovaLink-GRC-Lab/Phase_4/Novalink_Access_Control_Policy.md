## 1. Purpose
This Access Control Policy defines the rules and requirements for
managing access to Novalink Technologies Ltd information systems,
data and physical locations. It ensures that access to sensitive
information is granted only to authorized individuals based on
business need and principle of least privilege.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All Novalink employees, contractors and third party vendors
- All information systems, applications and databases
- All customer and financial data processed by Novalink
- All Novalink offices in Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya
- All cloud based systems and remote access connections

---

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Access Control Principles
Novalink will manage access to its systems and data based on the
following principles:

- Least Privilege: Users are granted only the minimum access
  required to perform their job function
- Need to Know: Access to sensitive data is restricted to those
  with a legitimate business need
- Separation of Duties: Critical functions are divided among
  multiple individuals to prevent fraud and error
- Zero Trust: No user or system is trusted by default regardless
  of their location or network

### 3.2 User Access Management
- All user accounts must be formally requested and approved by
  the relevant department manager
- New user accounts will be created by IT Support following
  written approval from the IT Manager
- Each user must have a unique account and must never share
  their credentials with any other person
- Generic or shared accounts are strictly prohibited
- User accounts must be disabled immediately upon termination
  of employment or contract

### 3.3 Access Levels and Classification
Novalink defines the following access levels:

| Access Level | Description | Authorized Users |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 - Public | General company information | All employees |
| Level 2 - Internal | Internal business data | All employees |
| Level 3 - Confidential | Sensitive business data | Authorized staff only |
| Level 4 - Restricted | Customer PII and financial data | Named individuals only |

### 3.4 Privileged Access Management
- Privileged accounts such as system administrator accounts
  must be strictly controlled and documented
- Privileged access must be approved by the CISO
- All privileged account activity must be logged and monitored
  by the SOC team
- Privileged accounts must not be used for routine daily tasks
- Multi Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all
  privileged accounts

### 3.5 Remote Access
- Remote access to Novalink systems is permitted only through
  the approved VPN solution
- All remote access sessions must use Multi Factor Authentication
- Remote access rights must be approved by the IT Manager
- Remote access sessions will be monitored by the SOC team
- Inactive remote sessions will be automatically terminated
  after 15 minutes

### 3.6 Third Party Access
- Third party vendors requiring access to Novalink systems must
  sign a formal Access Agreement before access is granted
- Third party access must be time limited and reviewed monthly
- All third party access must be approved by the GRC Manager
  and IT Manager
- Third party access will be monitored and logged at all times
- Access will be revoked immediately upon completion of work

### 3.7 Access Review
- All user access rights will be reviewed quarterly by the
  IT Manager and GRC Manager
- Department managers must confirm the access rights of their
  team members during each review
- Access rights that are no longer required must be revoked
  immediately
- Results of access reviews must be documented and retained
  for audit purposes

### 3.8 Physical Access Control
- Access to Novalink server rooms and data centers is restricted
  to authorized IT staff only
- Physical access logs must be maintained and reviewed monthly
- Visitors must be accompanied by an authorized Novalink employee
  at all times in secure areas

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| CISO | Own and enforce this policy |
| IT Manager | Implement and manage all access control systems |
| IT Support | Create, modify and disable user accounts |
| GRC Manager | Monitor compliance and conduct access reviews |
| HR Manager | Notify IT of new starters and leavers immediately |
| Department Managers | Approve access requests for their teams |
| SOC Team | Monitor privileged and remote access activity |
| Internal Audit | Independently verify access control compliance |

---

## 5. Compliance and Enforcement
- All employees must comply with this policy at all times
- Violations including sharing credentials or unauthorized access
  attempts will result in disciplinary action
- Serious violations may be reported to regulatory authorities
  including CBN and NDPC
- The GRC Manager will conduct quarterly compliance reviews
  and report findings to the CISO

---

## 6. Regulatory Alignment
This policy aligns with the following regulations and frameworks:
- Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) — Article 2.6
- CBN Cybersecurity Framework — Access Control Domain
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Annex A Control 5.15 to 5.18
- CIS Controls v8 — Control 5 and Control 6

---

## 7. Review and Update Schedule
- This policy will be reviewed annually by the GRC Manager and CISO
- The policy will also be reviewed following any major security
  incident, regulatory change or significant organizational change
- All updates must be approved by the CISO and communicated
  to all staff
