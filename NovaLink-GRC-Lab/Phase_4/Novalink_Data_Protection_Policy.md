## 1. Purpose
This Data Protection Policy defines how Novalink Technologies Ltd
collects, processes, stores, shares and disposes of personal data
in compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations.
It ensures that all personal data is handled with the highest
standards of care, integrity and security.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All Novalink employees, contractors and third party vendors
- All personal data collected from customers, employees and partners
- All systems and processes used to handle personal data
- All Novalink offices in Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya
- All cloud based systems processing Novalink data

---

## 3. Applicable Regulations
Novalink operates across three countries and is subject to the
following data protection regulations and frameworks:

### Nigeria
- Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019
- Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023
- CBN Cybersecurity Framework 2022
- CBN Consumer Protection Framework
- CBN Risk Based Cybersecurity Framework
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS v4.0)

### Ghana
- Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)
- Bank of Ghana Cybersecurity Directive 2018

### Kenya
- Data Protection Act 2019
- Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) Cybersecurity Guidelines

### International
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022
- CIS Controls v8
- PCI DSS v4.0

## 4. Policy Statement

### 4.1 Data Protection Principles
Novalink is committed to processing personal data in accordance
with the following principles:

- Lawfulness: Data is collected and processed on a lawful basis
- Purpose Limitation: Data is collected for specified and
  legitimate purposes only
- Data Minimization: Only data that is necessary is collected
- Accuracy: Personal data is kept accurate and up to date
- Storage Limitation: Data is retained only as long as necessary
- Integrity and Confidentiality: Data is protected against
  unauthorized access, loss or destruction
- Accountability: Novalink is responsible for demonstrating
  compliance with these principles

### 4.2 Lawful Basis for Processing
Novalink will only process personal data where one of the
following lawful bases applies:
- Consent: The data subject has given clear consent
- Contract: Processing is necessary to fulfill a contract
- Legal Obligation: Processing is required by law
- Legitimate Interest: Processing is necessary for Novalinks
  legitimate business interests

### 4.3 Data Subject Rights
Novalink recognizes and will uphold the following rights of
data subjects across all three operating countries:

| Right | Description | Response Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Right of Access | Request a copy of their personal data | 30 days |
| Right to Rectification | Request correction of inaccurate data | 30 days |
| Right to Erasure | Request deletion of their personal data | 30 days |
| Right to Restriction | Request restriction of processing | 30 days |
| Right to Portability | Request transfer of their data | 30 days |
| Right to Object | Object to processing of their data | 30 days |

### 4.4 Data Classification and Handling

| Data Type | Classification | Handling Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Customer PII | Restricted | Encrypted at rest and in transit |
| KYC Documents | Restricted | Stored in secure systems with limited access |
| Financial Records | Confidential | Encrypted and access controlled |
| Employee Data | Confidential | HR system with restricted access |
| Business Data | Internal | Standard access controls apply |

### 4.5 Data Retention
- Customer data will be retained for 7 years in line with
  CBN requirements
- Employee data will be retained for 5 years after termination
- KYC documents will be retained for 5 years after account closure
- Ghanaian customer data will be retained in line with the
  Data Protection Act 2012 requirements
- Kenyan customer data will be retained in line with the
  Data Protection Act 2019 requirements
- All data must be securely disposed of after the retention
  period using approved methods

### 4.6 Data Transfers
- Personal data must not be transferred outside Nigeria without
  appropriate safeguards in place
- Cross border transfers between Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya must
  comply with applicable local data protection laws in each country
- All international data transfers must be approved by the
  Legal and Compliance team
- Data Processing Agreements must be in place for all third
  party data processors
- Transfers to countries outside Africa must comply with GDPR
  adequacy requirements where EU data subjects are involved

### 4.7 Data Breach Management
- All personal data breaches must be reported to the GRC Manager
  and CISO immediately upon discovery
- Breaches affecting Nigerian data subjects must be reported to
  the NDPC within 72 hours of discovery
- Breaches affecting Ghanaian data subjects must be reported to
  the Data Protection Commission of Ghana
- Breaches affecting Kenyan data subjects must be reported to
  the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner of Kenya
- Affected data subjects must be notified without undue delay
  where the breach poses a high risk to their rights
- All breaches must be documented in the incident register

### 4.8 Privacy by Design
- Data protection considerations must be incorporated into all
  new systems and processes from the outset
- Privacy Impact Assessments must be conducted for any new
  processing activity involving sensitive personal data
- The GRC Manager must be consulted on all new data processing
  activities before they commence

### 4.9 Country Specific Obligations

#### Nigeria
- Novalink must register with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission
- Annual data protection audits must be conducted and submitted
  to the NDPC
- A Data Protection Officer must be appointed

#### Ghana
- Novalink must register with the Data Protection Commission
  of Ghana
- Data processing must comply with the Data Protection Act 2012

#### Kenya
- Novalink must register with the Office of the Data Protection
  Commissioner of Kenya
- Data processing must comply with the Data Protection Act 2019

---

## 6. Training and Awareness
- All employees must complete data protection training upon
  joining Novalink
- Annual refresher training is mandatory for all staff
- Staff handling sensitive personal data must receive additional
  specialist training
- Training completion must be documented and retained

---

## 7. Compliance and Enforcement
- Violations of this policy will result in disciplinary action
- Serious violations may be reported to the NDPC and other
  relevant regulatory authorities in Ghana and Kenya
- The GRC Manager will conduct quarterly compliance reviews
  and report findings to the CISO
- Non compliance with NDPR can result in fines of up to
  2 percent of annual gross revenue
- Non compliance with GDPR can result in fines of up to
  4 percent of global annual turnover

---

## 8. Review and Update Schedule
- This policy will be reviewed annually by the GRC Manager and CISO
- The policy will also be reviewed following any regulatory change
  in Nigeria, Ghana or Kenya
- All updates must be approved by the CISO and communicated
  to all staff
