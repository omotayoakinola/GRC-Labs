## 1. Purpose
This Incident Response Policy defines the process for identifying,
managing and resolving information security incidents at Novalink
Technologies Ltd. It ensures that security incidents are handled
in a timely, consistent and effective manner to minimize business
impact and meet regulatory reporting obligations.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All Novalink employees, contractors and third party vendors
- All information systems, networks and devices used by Novalink
- All security incidents affecting Novalink data or operations
- All Novalink offices in Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya
- All cloud based systems and remote working environments

---

## 3. Incident Classification

### 3.1 Incident Severity Levels

| Severity | Level | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | P1 | Severe impact on business operations or data | Ransomware attack, major data breach |
| High | P2 | Significant impact on systems or data | Unauthorized access to customer data |
| Medium | P3 | Moderate impact with limited scope | Malware detected on single device |
| Low | P4 | Minimal impact on operations | Phishing email reported by staff |

---

## 4. Policy Statement

### 4.1 Incident Response Principles
Novalink is committed to responding to security incidents based
on the following principles:

- Speed: Incidents must be identified and contained as quickly
  as possible to minimize damage
- Consistency: All incidents must follow this defined response
  process regardless of severity
- Documentation: All incident details and response actions must
  be fully documented
- Communication: Relevant stakeholders must be notified promptly
  and appropriately
- Improvement: Every incident must result in lessons learned to
  prevent recurrence

### 4.2 Incident Response Phases

#### Phase 1 — Preparation
- Maintain an up to date incident response plan and contact list
- Conduct regular incident response training for SOC team
- Ensure all monitoring and detection tools are operational
- Define and maintain escalation procedures

#### Phase 2 — Identification
- Monitor systems continuously for signs of security incidents
- Investigate all alerts and anomalies reported by staff or tools
- Classify the incident according to the severity levels above
- Document initial findings including time, nature and scope

#### Phase 3 — Containment
- Isolate affected systems to prevent further spread
- Preserve evidence for forensic investigation
- Implement temporary fixes to limit damage
- Notify relevant stakeholders based on severity level

#### Phase 4 — Eradication
- Identify and remove the root cause of the incident
- Remove malware, unauthorized access or other threats
- Apply security patches and updates as required
- Verify that all traces of the threat have been eliminated

#### Phase 5 — Recovery
- Restore affected systems from clean backups
- Verify that systems are functioning normally before returning
  to production
- Monitor restored systems closely for signs of reinfection
- Document all recovery actions taken

#### Phase 6 — Lessons Learned
- Conduct a post incident review within 5 business days
- Document what happened, what was done and what can be improved
- Update the incident response plan based on findings
- Share lessons learned with relevant stakeholders

### 4.3 Regulatory Reporting Obligations
Novalink must report certain incidents to regulators within
defined timeframes:

| Regulator | Incident Type | Reporting Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| NDPC | Personal data breach | Within 72 hours of discovery |
| CBN | Significant cyber incident | Within 24 hours of discovery |
| Internal Board | Critical P1 incidents | Within 2 hours of discovery |

### 4.4 Incident Communication
- P1 Critical incidents must be escalated to the CISO and CEO
  immediately
- P2 High incidents must be escalated to the CISO within 1 hour
- P3 Medium incidents must be reported to the SOC Manager
  within 4 hours
- P4 Low incidents must be logged and reported to SOC Manager
  within 24 hours
- External communications regarding incidents must be approved
  by the CEO and Legal and Compliance team before release

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| CISO | Own the incident response program and approve major decisions |
| SOC Manager | Lead incident response operations and coordinate the team |
| SOC Analyst | Detect, investigate and contain security incidents |
| IT Manager | Provide technical support during incident response |
| GRC Manager | Ensure regulatory reporting obligations are met |
| Legal and Compliance | Advise on legal obligations and external communications |
| HR Manager | Support incidents involving employee misconduct |
| CEO | Approve external communications for critical incidents |
| All Employees | Report suspected incidents immediately to the SOC |

---

## 6. Incident Reporting
- All employees must report suspected security incidents immediately
  by contacting the SOC team
- Reports can be made via email, phone or the internal ticketing
  system
- No employee will face disciplinary action for reporting a
  suspected incident in good faith
- False reports made deliberately will be subject to disciplinary
  action

---

## 7. Evidence Handling
- All incident evidence must be preserved in its original state
- Evidence must be collected and stored securely by the SOC team
- Chain of custody must be maintained for all evidence
- Evidence must be retained for a minimum of 3 years in line
  with CBN requirements

---

## 8. Compliance and Enforcement
- All employees must comply with this policy and report incidents
  immediately
- Failure to report a known incident is a serious violation of
  this policy
- Deliberate concealment of an incident will result in immediate
  termination and possible legal action
- The GRC Manager will review incident logs quarterly and report
  to the CISO

---

## 9. Regulatory Alignment
This policy aligns with the following regulations and frameworks:
- Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) — Article 2.9
- CBN Cybersecurity Framework — Incident Response Domain
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Annex A Control 5.24 to 5.28
- CIS Controls v8 — Control 17
