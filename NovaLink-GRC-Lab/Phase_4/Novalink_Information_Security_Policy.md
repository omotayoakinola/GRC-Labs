## 1. Purpose
This Information Security Policy establishes the overarching framework
for protecting the confidentiality, integrity and availability of
information assets at Novalink Technologies Ltd. It defines the
principles and responsibilities that govern how information security
is managed across the organization.

This policy is the master security document from which all other
security policies, standards and procedures are derived.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All Novalink employees, contractors and third party vendors
- All information assets owned or managed by Novalink
- All systems, networks and devices used to process Novalink data
- All Novalink offices in Nigeria, Ghana and Kenya
- All cloud services and third party platforms used by Novalink

---

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Information Security Principles
Novalink is committed to protecting its information assets in accordance
with the following core principles:

- Confidentiality: Information is accessible only to those authorized
  to access it
- Integrity: Information is accurate, complete and protected from
  unauthorized modification
- Availability: Information and systems are available to authorized
  users when needed
- Accountability: All users are accountable for their actions on
  Novalink systems
- Compliance: Novalink will comply with all applicable laws, regulations
  and contractual obligations

### 3.2 Security Governance
- The Board of Directors maintains ultimate accountability for
  information security at Novalink
- The CISO is responsible for the design, implementation and oversight
  of the information security program
- All department heads are responsible for ensuring their teams comply
  with this policy and all related security policies

### 3.3 Risk Management
- Novalink will conduct formal risk assessments at least annually
- All identified risks will be documented in a risk register
- Risk treatment decisions will be approved by the CISO and reported
  to the Board
- Residual risks must be formally accepted by the CISO or CEO

### 3.4 Asset Management
- All information assets must be identified, classified and documented
- Assets must be assigned an owner who is responsible for their
  protection
- Information must be classified as Public, Internal, Confidential
  or Restricted based on its sensitivity

### 3.5 Access Control
- Access to information and systems will be granted on a need to know
  and least privilege basis
- All access rights must be formally approved by the relevant manager
- Access rights will be reviewed quarterly and revoked immediately
  upon termination of employment or contract

### 3.6 Incident Management
- All security incidents must be reported immediately to the SOC
- The SOC Manager is responsible for coordinating incident response
- All incidents must be documented and lessons learned must be captured
- Reportable incidents will be disclosed to regulators within
  timeframes required by CBN and NDPC

### 3.7 Third Party Security
- All third party vendors with access to Novalink systems or data
  must sign a Data Processing Agreement
- Third party security controls will be assessed before onboarding
- Vendor access will be monitored and reviewed annually

### 3.8 Regulatory Compliance
- Novalink will maintain compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection
  Regulation (NDPR)
- Novalink will comply with the CBN Cybersecurity Framework
- Where applicable Novalink will align with ISO/IEC 27001:2022
  and CIS Controls v8

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Board of Directors | Ultimate accountability for information security |
| CEO | Champion security culture at executive level |
| CISO | Own and oversee the information security program |
| GRC Manager | Maintain and update this policy annually |
| All Employees | Read, understand and comply with this policy |
| IT Manager | Implement technical controls to support this policy |
| Internal Audit | Independently verify compliance with this policy |

---

## 5. Compliance and Enforcement
- All employees and contractors must read and acknowledge this policy
  upon joining Novalink
- Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to
  and including termination of employment or contract
- Serious violations may be reported to relevant regulatory authorities
- The GRC Manager will monitor compliance and report findings to
  the CISO quarterly
